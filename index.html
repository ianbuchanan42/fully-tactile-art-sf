Fully Tactile Art SF

Upcoming Event: Staying in "Touch"

Date: March 9th, 2025

Location: Jettison Creative
47 Jeff Adachi Way, San Francisco.

Time: 12:00–3:00 pm (Presentations begin at 1:00 pm)

This small scale and intimate exhibition will showcase the work of 5 artists, some of which displayed work last year and some that are new to our programming.

There will be a presentation by the artists in conversation with Fully Tactile Art SF's project directors beginning at 1:00 pm. Discussions will follow the importance of accessibility in art exhibitions and how the creative process of the artists can be influenced by this work.

The Artists

  • Tan Reese: Selling beautiful usable ceramic pieces full of whimsy.
  • Susan Dambroff: Enchanting us with her poetry and voice.
  • Carolyn CC Hart: Delighting us with her research into pain forms.
  • Kit Rosefield: Telling us stories through audio and touch.
  • Blink Pop Shift: Sharing with us a library of holdable, touchable sculptures that you can borrow, take home, and return like a book from the library. Each sculpture was created by San Francisco artist M Eilo to document the physical and emotional sensations of disability. The pieces were all made in the artist's home and they are excited to bring work from their home to yours. You can sign up to borrow from the art library at this event!

This is an incredible opportunity to meet tactile artists and to hear more about the importance of Fully Tactile Art SF's work in curating artistic spaces that are accessible to more people, so please join us on March 9th!

Why Tactile Art?

This project is a response to the continuous trend of art being enjoyed through vision, instead of also being experienced through touch and other senses. We aim to create a space for artists and patrons to explore what it truly means to "see" art beyond the visual.
